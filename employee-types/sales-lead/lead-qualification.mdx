## Overview

Converting raw leads into qualified opportunities requires deep research—company background, tech stack, funding, buying signals, and decision-maker identification. Righthands automate this research, providing comprehensive prospect profiles that help you prioritize outreach and personalize conversations.

## Research Data Sources

### Company Information
- **Public Data**: LinkedIn, company websites, Crunchbase, financial filings
- **News & PR**: Press releases, funding announcements, leadership changes
- **Social Media**: Company and executive LinkedIn activity, Twitter engagement
- **Job Postings**: Open roles indicating growth areas and pain points

### Technical Intelligence
- **Tech Stack Detection**: BuiltWith, Wappalyzer, security certificates
- **GitHub Activity**: Open source usage, engineering team size and quality
- **API Documentation**: Integration capabilities and technical sophistication
- **Product Reviews**: G2, Capterra for competitor usage and satisfaction

### Buying Signals
- **Funding Events**: New capital raises indicating budget availability
- **Expansion Indicators**: New offices, headcount growth, market entry
- **Leadership Changes**: New executives often bring new vendor relationships
- **Pain Point Signals**: Job postings, social complaints, competitive research

## Lead Scoring Framework

### Qualification Criteria Setup

Define your scoring model for Righthand to evaluate:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Fit Score (40%)">
    - Company size: 50-500 employees (10 points)
    - Industry: B2B SaaS, FinTech (10 points)
    - Revenue: $5M-$50M ARR (10 points)
    - Tech stack: Uses Salesforce, AWS (10 points)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Intent Score (30%)">
    - Recent funding: Series A-C (10 points)
    - Active hiring: Data/Engineering roles (10 points)
    - Competitor usage: Using dated solution (10 points)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Accessibility Score (30%)">
    - Warm intro available (15 points)
    - Decision maker identified on LinkedIn (10 points)
    - Recent engagement with content (5 points)
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Total Score**: 100 points → 80+ = High Priority, 60-79 = Medium, below 60 = Nurture

## Best Practices

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define Your ICP Precisely">
    The more specific your ideal customer profile, the more accurate Righthand's qualification will be. Include must-haves and nice-to-haves.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Validate Research Samples">
    Review initial research batches to ensure quality and adjust criteria. Provide feedback on what information is most valuable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Score Thresholds">
    Establish clear score ranges for high/medium/low priority. Adjust based on your capacity and close rates.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update Regularly">
    Markets change. Revisit your ICP and scoring criteria quarterly to stay aligned with business goals.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Combine Signals">
    Best qualification combines fit (who they are), intent (what they need), and timing (when they'll buy).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Metrics to Track

Monitor these metrics to optimize lead qualification:

- **Research Quality Score**: Accuracy of company data and decision makers
- **Qualification to Opportunity Rate**: % of qualified leads that become opportunities
- **Score Correlation**: How well lead scores predict actual conversion
- **Research Time Savings**: Hours saved per lead vs. manual research
- **Pipeline Velocity**: Time from lead to qualified opportunity
