## Overview

Walking into sales calls unprepared wastes your time and the prospect's time. Righthands compile comprehensive pre-call research briefs—company background, stakeholder intel, pain points, competitive landscape, and recommended talking points—so you can focus on building relationships and closing deals.

## Common Use Cases

### Discovery Call Preparation

<CodeGroup>
```text Request
"I have a discovery call tomorrow at 2pm with Acme Corp - Sarah Chen
(CTO) and Mike Rodriguez (VP Engineering). Prepare a comprehensive
brief including company background, their tech challenges, talking
points, and discovery questions."
```

```text Righthand Brief
MEETING BRIEF: Acme Corp Discovery Call
Date: Tomorrow 2pm | Duration: 30 minutes
Attendees: Sarah Chen (CTO), Mike Rodriguez (VP Engineering)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Acme Corp is a 150-person B2B SaaS company ($20M ARR) facing data
integration challenges as they scale. Recent Series B funding ($15M)
suggests budget availability. Strong fit for our enterprise tier.

KEY OPPORTUNITY: Currently using legacy ETL tools (DataOld), contract
renewal in Q2. VP Engineering recently posted about "data pipeline
complexity" on LinkedIn.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

COMPANY OVERVIEW
- Founded: 2019 | Employees: 150 | Revenue: ~$20M ARR
- Recent funding: $15M Series B (Dec 2025)
- Growth: 40% YoY, recently expanded to UK market
- Tech Stack: AWS, PostgreSQL, Salesforce, Segment, DataOld (ETL)
- Customers: 500+ mid-market companies in FinTech/Insurance

RECENT NEWS
- Series B announcement (Dec 2025) - expanding product and team
- Opened London office (Nov 2025) - international expansion
- Launched new enterprise tier (Oct 2025) - moving upmarket

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ATTENDEE PROFILES

Sarah Chen - CTO
- Background: Previously Sr. Engineer at Stripe (5 years)
- At Acme: 2 years, promoted to CTO 6 months ago
- Priorities: Infrastructure scale, team efficiency, security
- Recent activity: Posted about hiring 10 engineers in Q1
- Communication style: Technical, data-driven, values efficiency
- Personal: Stanford CS, active in Women in Tech community

Mike Rodriguez - VP Engineering
- Background: Engineering Lead at DocuSign (3 years)
- At Acme: 1.5 years, managing team of 25 engineers
- Priorities: Developer productivity, reducing technical debt
- Recent activity: LinkedIn post: "Spending too much time on
  data pipeline maintenance instead of product features"
- Communication style: Pragmatic, ROI-focused, hands-on
- Personal: UC Berkeley, contributes to open source

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

IDENTIFIED PAIN POINTS

1. DATA INTEGRATION COMPLEXITY (High Priority)
   - Using DataOld (legacy ETL), maintenance-heavy
   - 6 open "Data Engineer" positions → scaling challenges
   - Mike's LinkedIn: "pipeline maintenance" pain point

2. INTERNATIONAL EXPANSION (Medium Priority)
   - New UK office needs data synced across regions
   - Compliance concerns (GDPR) with current setup

3. TECHNICAL DEBT (Medium Priority)
   - Legacy ETL not keeping pace with growth
   - Contract renewal Q2 → window of opportunity

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
- Currently using: DataOld (legacy ETL tool)
- Contract expires: Q2 2026 (perfect timing)
- Pain points: High maintenance, limited scaling, no modern features
- Likely competitors: Fivetran, Airbyte (may already be evaluating)

DISPLACEMENT STRATEGY
→ Focus on time-to-value: "Our customers reduce integration
   maintenance by 70% in first month"
→ Emphasize modern architecture vs. legacy DataOld
→ Stress international data compliance capabilities

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

RECOMMENDED AGENDA (30 minutes)

Minutes 0-5: INTRO & AGENDA
- Thank them for their time
- "I've done some homework on Acme - exciting Series B and UK
  expansion. I'd love to understand your data infrastructure
  challenges and see if there's a fit."

Minutes 5-15: DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
1. "Walk me through your current data integration setup. What's
   working well, and where are the pain points?"

2. "Mike, I saw your post about pipeline maintenance. What % of
   engineering time goes to data work vs. product features?"

3. "With the UK expansion, how are you handling cross-region
   data sync and compliance?"

4. "What's your evaluation timeline? I noticed DataOld contract
   renewal is coming up."

5. "Sarah, as you scale the team from 25 to 35 engineers in Q1,
   what infrastructure improvements are priorities?"

Minutes 15-25: VALUE PROP & CASE STUDY
- Tailor pitch based on their responses
- Share case study: "TechFlow (similar B2B SaaS, also scaled from
  $20M→$40M) reduced data engineering time 70%, freed up 3 FTEs
  for product work"
- Demo key feature: International compliance capabilities

Minutes 25-30: NEXT STEPS
- Technical deep dive with engineering team?
- Proof of concept / trial evaluation?
- Commercial conversation with CFO?

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ANTICIPATED OBJECTIONS & RESPONSES

"We're already evaluating Fivetran"
→ "That's great - you're clearly taking this seriously. Many
   customers evaluate us alongside Fivetran. Can I ask what's
   most important in your decision criteria? [Then differentiate
   on their stated priorities]"

"Budget is tight post-fundraise"
→ "I totally understand. Most customers see ROI within 60 days
   through engineering time savings. TechFlow freed up 3 FTE
   worth of capacity - more than paying for our solution."

"We've got DataOld contract until Q2"
→ "Perfect timing actually. Gives us Q1 to run a proof of concept
   so you can make an informed renewal decision with real data."

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

RELEVANT CASE STUDIES

1. TechFlow Solutions (Most Relevant)
   - Similar: B2B SaaS, $20M→$40M scale, international expansion
   - Challenge: Legacy ETL couldn't scale with growth
   - Result: 70% reduction in pipeline maintenance, 3 FTE capacity

2. DataCore Systems
   - Similar: Series B funded, AWS infrastructure
   - Challenge: GDPR compliance for EU expansion
   - Result: Achieved compliance in 2 weeks vs. 6-month estimate

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

POST-CALL ACTIONS
- Send follow-up email within 2 hours with:
  - Meeting summary & key discussion points
  - Relevant case studies mentioned
  - Proposed next steps with dates
- Update CRM with detailed notes
- If technical deep dive requested → coordinate with Solutions team
```

## Research Intelligence Sources

### Company-Level Intelligence

**Financial & Growth Data:**
- Revenue estimates, funding rounds, growth trajectory
- Public financial statements (if applicable)
- Analyst reports and market position

**Strategic Initiatives:**
- Recent press releases and company announcements
- Executive interviews and conference presentations
- Product roadmap signals from job postings and acquisitions

**Operational Context:**
- Office locations and expansion activity
- Headcount growth patterns by department
- Recent organizational changes

### Stakeholder-Level Intelligence

**Professional Background:**
```text Example Insights
Sarah Chen (CTO):
- 5 years at Stripe: Likely values reliability, scale, developer experience
- Promoted recently: Proving herself, may be budget-conscious
- Stanford CS: Technical depth, appreciates elegant architecture
- Women in Tech advocate: Values inclusive vendors and diverse teams
```

**Communication Patterns:**
- LinkedIn activity: What topics do they engage with?
- Conference talks: What challenges do they discuss publicly?
- Blog posts / articles: What's their thought leadership focus?
- Twitter/social: Communication style and current priorities

**Recent Activity & Triggers:**
- New in role (30/60/90 days): Often bring new vendor relationships
- Company achievements: Funding, awards, growth milestones
- Pain point signals: Public complaints or challenges mentioned
- Network connections: Mutual connections for warm intros

### Question Trees

Righthand builds decision-tree question frameworks:

```text Example Framework
OPENING QUESTION:
"Walk me through your current data integration process."

→ IF they mention manual work:
  FOLLOW-UP: "How much engineering time goes into maintenance?"
  → IF significant time mentioned:
    VALUE PROP: Time savings & automation

→ IF they mention reliability issues:
  FOLLOW-UP: "What happens when pipelines fail?"
  → IF customer impact mentioned:
    VALUE PROP: Reliability & SLAs

→ IF they mention scaling challenges:
  FOLLOW-UP: "What's driving the need to scale?"
  → IF growth mentioned:
    VALUE PROP: Elastic scaling, no maintenance overhead
```

## Best Practices

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare 24 Hours in Advance">
    Request research briefs at least 24 hours before calls to allow time for review and customization.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Focus on Stakeholder Priorities">
    Different attendees care about different things. Tailor your approach to each person on the call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update CRM Pre-Call">
    Ensure Righthand has access to all previous interactions and notes to build on past conversations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customize, Don't Script">
    Use preparation as a guide, not a script. Stay flexible and responsive to the actual conversation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Track What Works">
    Note which research insights, questions, and talking points lead to successful outcomes. Refine over time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Integration with Sales Process

### Pre-Call → Call → Post-Call Flow

**Morning of Call:**
1. "Prepare brief for 2pm Acme Corp call"
2. Review research during lunch
3. Enter call confident and informed

**During Call:**
4. Reference research insights naturally
5. Take high-level notes, stay engaged

**Post-Call:**
6. "Draft follow-up email based on these discussion points"
7. "Update CRM with call notes and next steps"
8. "Schedule technical deep dive with their engineering team"

## Metrics to Track

### Preparation Quality
- **Research Accuracy**: How often are research insights accurate and relevant?
- **Stakeholder Insight Value**: Do stakeholder profiles improve conversation quality?
- **Question Effectiveness**: Which discovery questions uncover the most useful information?

### Call Outcomes
- **Meeting-to-Opportunity Rate**: % of prepared calls that advance to opportunities
- **Decision Maker Access**: Success rate getting right stakeholders on calls
- **Follow-up Response Rate**: % of follow-up emails that get replies

### Time Efficiency
- **Prep Time Savings**: 2-3 hours of manual research → 15 min review
- **Call Confidence**: Subjective rating of how prepared you feel
- **Follow-up Speed**: Time from call end to follow-up sent (under 2 hours target)
