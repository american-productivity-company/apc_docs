## Overview

Your Righthand can act as an AI receptionist — screening incoming calls, filtering out spam, and making sure important contacts always reach you. Forward your calls to your Righthand and it will intelligently handle each one based on who's calling and your preferences.

## How Call Screening Works

When a call comes in, your Righthand follows this process:

1. **Priority contacts** — Recognized VIP contacts ring through to you immediately.
2. **Known contacts** — Handled according to your preferences (forward, take a message, etc.).
3. **Unknown numbers** — Your Righthand engages the caller to assess legitimacy before deciding how to handle it.
4. **Spam and robocalls** — Automatically dismissed.

Your Righthand uses calendar awareness, contact history, and personal context to respond appropriately to every call.

## Prerequisites

Before setting up call forwarding, make sure you have:
- A Righthand with a phone number assigned (this is set up automatically when you hire one)
- Access to your phone's settings or carrier account
- Your Righthand's phone number (found on their detail page)

## Setting Up Call Forwarding (iPhone)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Disable Live Voicemail">
    Go to **Settings → Phone → Live Voicemail** and turn it off. This prevents your phone from intercepting calls before they can be forwarded to your Righthand.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Turn off immediate call forwarding">
    If you have unconditional call forwarding enabled, turn it off so you can set up conditional forwarding instead.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable conditional forwarding">
    Use your carrier's forwarding codes to route unanswered, busy, or unreachable calls to your Righthand's phone number.

    <AccordionGroup>
      <Accordion title="AT&T">
        Dial `*004*[Righthand Phone Number]*11#` and press call.
      </Accordion>
      <Accordion title="Verizon">
        Dial `*71[Righthand Phone Number]` and press call.
      </Accordion>
      <Accordion title="T-Mobile">
        Dial `**61*[Righthand Phone Number]#` and press call.
      </Accordion>
    </AccordionGroup>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up a Focus mode (optional)">
    Create an iPhone Focus mode that limits notifications to your VIP contacts. This works well alongside call forwarding — important calls ring through while everything else goes to your Righthand.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test it out">
    Have someone call you from a known number and an unknown number to verify that calls are being forwarded and screened correctly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Advanced Features

Your Righthand gets smarter about call handling over time. Key capabilities include:

- **Relationship awareness** — Knows how to handle calls differently for family, colleagues, and service providers.
- **Calendar integration** — Responds with context about your availability and schedule.
- **Contact history** — References past interactions for continuity across calls.
- **Custom instructions** — Set specific rules for how certain types of calls should be handled.
- **Follow-up actions** — Can send emails, create tasks, or schedule callbacks after a call.

## Best Practices

- **Define your VIP list** — Make sure your most important contacts are marked so they always ring through.
- **Keep your context updated** — The more your Righthand knows about your schedule and preferences, the better it handles calls.
- **Review call summaries** — Check in on how calls are being handled and provide feedback to improve screening.
- **Keep Focus mode active** — If you've set one up, leave it on during work hours for the best experience.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Calls aren't being forwarded">
    Make sure Live Voicemail is disabled and that conditional forwarding is set up with the correct carrier code. Try re-entering the forwarding code and testing with a call.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="All calls go straight to Righthand">
    You may have unconditional (immediate) forwarding enabled. Turn it off and set up conditional forwarding instead so you still receive calls from priority contacts.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Righthand isn't picking up forwarded calls">
    Verify your Righthand's phone number is correct in the forwarding settings. If the number is correct, reach out to support.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
